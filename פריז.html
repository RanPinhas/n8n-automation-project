Eiffel Tower - The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of Paris and one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. Built for the 1889 World's Fair, it was intended to be a temporary structure but has become a permanent part of the city's skyline. The tower stands at 324 meters tall and offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. Visitors can take a lift or climb the stairs to the top for an unforgettable experience. The Eiffel Tower is a must-visit attraction for anyone traveling to Paris.

Louvre Museum - The Louvre Museum is one of the world's largest and most famous museums, housing an impressive collection of art and artifacts from around the globe. The museum's origins date back to the 12th century, and it has been expanded and renovated numerous times over the centuries. The Louvre is home to some of the most famous works of art in history, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo. With its stunning architecture and vast collections, the Louvre is a must-visit destination for art lovers and history buffs. The museum's glass pyramid entrance, designed by I.M. Pei, is a iconic landmark in its own right. Visitors can spend hours exploring the museum's vast collections and exhibitions.

Notre Dame Cathedral - Notre Dame Cathedral is a beautiful and historic church that has been a part of Parisian life for over 850 years. The cathedral was built in the 12th century and features stunning Gothic architecture, including its famous rose windows and vaulted ceilings. Notre Dame has played a significant role in French history and has been the site of many important events, including coronations and royal weddings. The cathedral is also famous for its iconic bell towers and stunning views of the Seine River. Visitors can attend a mass or take a guided tour of the cathedral to learn more about its history and significance. Despite a devastating fire in 2019, Notre Dame remains one of Paris' most beloved and enduring landmarks.
